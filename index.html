FAQ

Questions We Hear Every Day

Everything you need to know before you get started. Can't find your answer? Get in touch and we'll respond within a few hours.

PDLC (Polymer Dispersed Liquid Crystal) technology is sandwiched between layers of glass or film. When power is off, the liquid crystals are randomly oriented, scattering light and creating a frosted, opaque appearance. When voltage is applied, the crystals align and the glass becomes clear — almost instantly. Switch time is under 0.01 seconds.
Yes — smart glass is clear when power is on and frosted when power is off. This means if there's a power outage, the glass defaults to frosted (private), which is the safer state for most applications. Power consumption is very low — approximately 3–5 watts per square metre.
Yes — our smart film is designed to be applied directly to existing glass using a self-adhesive cling technology. It can be installed without removing the glass from its frame in most cases. It works on flat glass surfaces and requires an electrician to connect the low-voltage transformer and wiring.
Smart glass works with a standard wall switch, remote control, smartphone app, voice assistants (Google Home, Alexa), and can be integrated into building management systems (BMS) for commercial projects. Each panel or zone can be controlled independently or grouped together.
Our PDLC smart glass is rated for over 100,000 switching cycles and a 10+ year functional lifespan. The film carries a 10-year manufacturer warranty. Smart glass panels carry a 5-year warranty on the PDLC function and a standard glass warranty for the glass itself.
When in the clear state, smart glass is fully transparent — similar to regular clear glass. When in the frosted state, it provides full privacy with a soft, milky white appearance. It blocks approximately 99% of UV light in both states. For applications that require privacy in both states, we offer tinted smart glass options.
