Answers

What your client will ask.

How long does it last?

Properly installed PDLC film has a manufacturer life expectation of 7–10 years indoors. Direct UV and moisture at unsealed edges are the two main failure modes — which is why every order ships with the edge-sealing kit.

Who wires it up?

The film runs at 60V AC (extra-low voltage). The 240V mains connection to the transformer must be done by a licensed electrician under AS/NZS 3000. Our controllers are RCM-marked and ready to install.

Can I retrofit it to existing glass?

Yes — that's the point. The film applies to existing glass (interior side) with its self-adhesive backing. Clean glass, careful application, sealed edges. Allow around 30 minutes per square metre for a careful installer.

What does the end client pay?

Typical Australian residential installs retail between $500–1,600 per square metre installed depending on scope and trade. Your margin sits in the middle and is yours to set.

What if a sheet arrives faulty?

Bench-test before install — apply to a temporary glass panel, power up. If the sheet has dead zones, inconsistent haze, or won't switch, photograph and email within 7 days of delivery. We replace.

Is it actually private when frosted?

Yes — visually opaque. Light and colour pass through but no detail or silhouette. Suitable for bathrooms, meeting rooms, medical and dental rooms, executive offices.
