# Smart Window Film — Australia Market Entry Plan

## 1. Executive Summary

**Opportunity:** The Australian PDLC smart film market is growing on the back of urban density (~90% urban population), hot climate, residential renovation spend, and commercial demand for flexible office partitioning. Wholesale film from Chinese factories costs roughly A$30–60/m²; finished, installed retail prices range A$500–1,600/m² — a structural margin that can support a real local business.

**Strategy:** Do **not** run a pure Alibaba dropship model. The product fails as a dropship category because every job needs custom sizing, Australian electrical compliance, skilled installation, and post-sale warranty support. Instead, run a **hybrid "Aussie-stocked + installed + warranted"** model: source film and components from vetted Chinese factories, hold inventory and do custom cutting in Australia, sell through a productised installation service, and build an installer/electrician partner network.

**12-month goal:** A$400k–600k revenue, gross margin 50–60%, 3 partner installers across one metro (Sydney or Melbourne), 80+ installed jobs, founding the brand for national expansion in Year 2.

## 2. Market Snapshot

### Demand drivers
- **Residential renovations** — bathrooms, ensuites, home offices, glass partitions, skylights.
- **Commercial fitouts** — meeting rooms, executive offices, medical clinics, dental, allied health, beauty/cosmetic clinics, co-working.
- **Hospitality** — hotels, spas, restaurant private dining, function rooms.
- **Specialist** — yachts, luxury vehicles, retail display windows, museums.
- **Climate fit** — hot summers, strong UV, energy-efficiency appetite. Smart film blocks ~99% UV and a meaningful share of IR, which sells in Australia.

### Market structure
- Fragmented: a handful of national-ish brands plus dozens of small installers and tinting shops cross-selling smart film.
- Most competitors lead with B2B (architects, builders, glaziers, fitout firms). The direct-to-consumer channel is underserved and noisy.
- Pricing is opaque — most sites force "request a quote", which creates room for a transparent, productised offer.

## 3. Why Pure Dropshipping Doesn't Work Here (and What To Do Instead)

### Why dropship fails
1. **Custom sizing** — film must be cut to each glass panel, often with cutouts for hardware. Stocking standard rolls in Australia and cutting locally is the only viable workflow.
2. **Electrical compliance** — the transformer/controller is mains-connected electrical equipment. Under Australian Consumer Law and the EESS (Electrical Equipment Safety System), it must be registered as a regulated equipment item and carry the **RCM mark**. Selling unmarked Chinese power supplies into AU is a regulatory and liability landmine.
3. **Installation skill** — dry-adhesive application requires technique. Edge sealing with conductive copper tape prevents delamination. Mains connection requires a **licensed electrician under AS/NZS 3000**. A bad install = a return = a refund + reputational damage.
4. **Lead time** — Air freight from China is 1–2 weeks plus customs; sea is 3–6 weeks. Customers comparing you to local stockists will walk.
5. **Warranty exposure** — under Australian Consumer Law, the **seller** carries the consumer guarantee regardless of where the goods came from. You cannot point at the Alibaba supplier when a film fails in year 2. You need stock you can audit and a warranty reserve.
6. **GST and import duty** — anything over A$1,000 attracts duty + 10% GST at the border; under A$1,000 still triggers GST. Reseller imports are claimable, but the cash-flow drag is real.

### The hybrid model

**Source in bulk → stock in Australia → cut to order → install with partners → own the warranty.**

This is what every credible competitor already does. Your edge is execution: better customer experience, faster turnaround, transparent pricing, and a sharper brand.

## 4. Recommended Business Model

### Product range (start narrow)
| SKU | Spec | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| Standard White Self-Adhesive PDLC | 0.4mm, ~88% light transmission clear, AC 48–65V | Bathrooms, partitions, residential |
| Premium Low-Haze PDLC | <5% haze, longer warranty | Executive offices, retail |
| Black/Tinted PDLC | For privacy + solar control | Hospitality, projector screens |
| Pre-cut DIY Kit (standard window sizes) | With controller, instructions | Online-only segment |
| Smart Controller (RCM-marked) | WiFi + RF, scene control | Sold with every job |

Hold 200–500m² of stock film + 30–50 controllers/transformers in a Sydney or Melbourne warehouse. Lock in a Chinese supplier who can ship RCM-ready componentry (or buy controllers locally from a compliant supplier).

### Service tiers
1. **Supply only** — pre-cut film + controller, shipped to customer or their installer. Lower margin, faster cash, online-first.
2. **Supply + install** — your team (or partner installer + electrician) does it. Fixed-price packages by window size.
3. **Commercial / trade** — quoted projects for builders, fitout companies, architects. Volume + repeat business.

### Pricing approach
Publish **fixed package prices** on the website. Most competitors hide pricing — transparent pricing is a wedge. Example structure:

- Single window (up to 1m²) supply + install: A$1,200–1,700
- Bathroom package (up to 3m²): A$2,800–3,800
- Office partition (per m² over 5m²): A$650–850
- Supply-only film: A$280–450/m² depending on grade

Hit ~8 jobs/week steady state and you're at ~A$1m revenue, ~A$500k gross.

## 5. Regulatory & Compliance Checklist

| Item | What's required | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| ABN + GST registration | ASIC / ATO | Mandatory once turnover >A$75k |
| Business name + trademark | IP Australia | Brand defence |
| Public liability insurance | A$10m–20m policy | Required by most commercial clients |
| Product liability insurance | Often bundled with PL | ACL exposure |
| EESS RCM registration | Register controllers/transformers as Level 2/3 electrical equipment | **Mandatory** to sell mains-connected gear |
| Licensed electrician engagement | Sub-contract or employ | Required for any 240V connection work |
| Customs broker | For bulk import | Saves hours and de-risks classification |
| GST + tariff on imports | 10% GST + applicable duty | Build into landed cost |
| Australian Consumer Law warranty | Minimum statutory rights | Build a warranty reserve (3–5% of revenue) |
| WorkCover | State-based | Required if hiring |

**Do not** sell a mains-connected transformer without RCM. Either source RCM-marked units from an Australian compliant supplier, or take responsibility yourself by registering as a Responsible Supplier on the EESS national database and getting your imported units tested by an accredited lab.

## 6. Sourcing Strategy

### What to ask your Alibaba supplier
- Minimum order quantity, lead time, payment terms (avoid 100% upfront — push for 30/70 or LC).
- Film roll specs: width (1.2m and 1.8m standard), max length, haze %, light transmission, driving voltage range, expected lifespan in cycles.
- Edge sealing — does film come pre-sealed or do you seal in-country?
- Defect rate and replacement policy in writing.
- Sample roll (5–10m) before any bulk order. Test for haze, switching speed, dead zones, edge stability.
- Compliance documentation: CE, RoHS, ISO 9001 minimum. RCM rarely available from China direct.

### Recommended sourcing posture
- **Two suppliers** from day one. Single-supplier risk on a product that can have factory-batch defects is too high.
- **Sea freight quarterly**, air freight only for urgent top-ups. Sea cuts unit cost ~50–70% vs air.
- **Pre-buy controllers from an Australian compliant distributor** for the first 6 months, even if margin is thinner — speed to market and compliance certainty outweigh the per-unit saving.

### Quality gate
Every roll gets bench-tested before it goes into a job: power up a test panel, check uniformity, check switching, check edges. Reject rate of 2–5% is normal — build it into your cost.

## 7. Go-to-Market

### Brand positioning
"The fastest, clearest, most reliable smart film install in Australia."

Implies:
- Published prices.
- Same-week scheduling for standard residential jobs.
- 5-year written warranty on film + 2 years on electronics (set a real reserve for this).
- Visible installer credentials and electrical compliance certificates.

### Channels (in order of priority for Year 1)

1. **SEO + Google Ads** — own the long tail. The competitors you listed rank for generic terms; you can win location + intent terms ("smart film bathroom Sydney", "switchable glass office Melbourne", "PDLC film cost Australia"). Budget A$3–5k/month from month 2.
2. **Instagram + TikTok before/after** — this product is visceral and demos brilliantly on video. One good viral install reel can replace months of ad spend. Budget: time, not money.
3. **Architect / builder / interior designer outreach** — they specify products. A 30-minute lunch + sample kit goes a long way. Aim for 5 specifier meetings/week in months 1–3.
4. **Real estate stylists & home stagers** — niche but high-leverage.
5. **Marketplace listings** — Amazon AU and eBay for the DIY pre-cut kit. Lower margin but builds reviews and brand search volume.
6. **Trade shows** — DesignBUILD, Sydney Build, HIA Home Shows. Year 2 onward.

### Sample/demo kit
Build 20 A4-sized demo panels with controllers. Every architect / builder / showroom gets one. The product sells itself in person.

### Reviews
Aim for 50 Google reviews in the first 6 months. Ask in-person at install handover with a QR code. Reviews are the single biggest conversion lever for a high-ticket home service.

## 10. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Supplier ships defective batch | Two suppliers, sample-test every roll, build 3–5% defect cost into pricing |
| Electrical compliance failure / ACCC action | Use RCM-marked components from day one OR complete EESS registration before selling |
| Customer botches DIY install and demands refund | Clear DIY kit T&Cs, video instructions, optional install upsell, capped refund policy |
| Installer/electrician quality variability | Standardise SOPs, photograph every install, post-install QC call within 48 hours |
| Currency / freight volatility | Hold 2–3 months of stock, quote with 30-day price validity |
| Competitor undercuts on price | Compete on speed + warranty + clarity, not price floor |
| Big brand (Gauzy, Switchglass) targets DTC | Move fast on brand + reviews while window is open |
| Founder bandwidth / burnout | Hire ops/admin help by month 6 if jobs >15/mo |

## 12. The One Strategic Bet

Most of your competitors are run by glaziers, tinters, or trade installers who think like tradespeople. The market is open to someone who **runs it like a productised consumer brand**: clear prices, fast booking, beautiful content, ruthless install standards, and a real warranty. That's the moat — not the film, which is a commodity. The film is just the substrate. The business is execution.
