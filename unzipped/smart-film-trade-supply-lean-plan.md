# Smart Film Trade Supply — Australia (Lean B2B Launch)

> A focused plan for a solo founder, A$20k starting capital, selling B2B to a mix of trades (window tinters, shopfitters, builders).

---

## 1. The Business in One Line

**You are a trade supply business.** You don't install. You don't market to homeowners. You stock one workhorse SKU plus the accessories that go with it, and you sell to tradies who already have customers. Your edge is reliability, fair trade pricing, fast turnaround, and the paperwork their clients ask for.

---

## 2. Realistic Constraints at A$20k Solo

You **cannot** at this budget:
- Self-register on the EESS as a Responsible Supplier (lab testing alone is A$3–5k+ and adds 6–10 weeks)
- Stock more than one or two SKUs deeply
- Rent a commercial warehouse
- Extend trade credit on day one
- Run a paid advertising spend big enough to move the needle in B2B
- Hire help in Year 1

You **can**:
- Pick one product, own it, and get known for it
- Buy AU-compliant controllers from a local wholesaler from day one
- Work from a garage, spare room, or A$80/month mini-storage
- Visit every target tradie in your metro in person, in 12 weeks
- Land 8–12 active trade accounts in 6 months
- Turn a profit by month 4–6 if you don't blow the buffer

---

## 3. The Product Range — Phase 1

Three SKUs. That's it.

| SKU | Spec | Wholesale to trade |
|---|---|---|
| Smart Film – Standard White Self-Adhesive PDLC | 1.2m × 30m roll, 0.4mm, ~88% LT, AC 48–65V drive | A$180–260/m² depending on tier |
| Smart Controller + Transformer | RCM-marked, AU-sourced, 60V output, remote + app | A$220–280/unit |
| Connection Kit | Copper tape, conductive ink, lead wires, install guide | A$30–45/kit |

No black film. No premium low-haze. No pre-cut DIY. No 1.8m rolls. Those come in Phase 2 once cash is flowing.

## 4. Target Customers — In Order

### Tier A: Window tinters (your priority — 70% of effort)
- Adjacent skill set; they already install film.
- Owner-operator decisions, fast yes/no.
- Buy by the roll, not the panel.
- Your highest-margin customer.
- **Find them:** Google Maps "window tinting [your city]", Instagram #windowtinter, ServiceSeeking, Hipages, Yellow Pages.
- **Target list:** 40–60 in your metro. Aim to win 8–12 repeat buyers.

### Tier B: Shopfitters & commercial fitout (20% of effort)
- Bigger jobs, project-based.
- Multiple stakeholders, longer sales cycle.
- Recurring through fitout cycles.
- **Find them:** Master Builders Association member list, LinkedIn search "shopfitter [city]", commercial fitout directories, asking architects who they use.
- **Target list:** 15–20. Win 3–5 active accounts.

### Tier C: Boutique builders & high-end reno (10% of effort)
- Slow to land, high lifetime value.
- Specifier influence — they tell their electrician what to buy.
- **Find them:** HIA member list, custom home magazines, Houzz, designer referrals.
- **Target list:** 10–15. Win 2–3.

**Why not electricians?** They're not buyers of the product — they're partners. Don't try to sell to them, recruit them as referral partners (give them a sample kit and a A$50 referral kickback for any successful sale).

## 8. Sourcing Strategy

### Film — Alibaba, always two suppliers
**What to demand on every order:**
- Width 1.2m, length 30m rolls (standard, easier to ship)
- 0.4mm thickness
- 88%+ light transmission in clear state
- Haze ≤5% in clear state
- Driving voltage 48–65V AC
- Pre-sealed edges (saves you sealing work)
- CE, RoHS, ISO 9001 documentation
- Defect replacement policy in writing
- Sample roll (5–10m) before any bulk order

**Payment:** Never 100% upfront. Push for 30/70 (30% deposit, 70% on shipping doc). If they refuse, walk.

**Test protocol** (do this on every batch — non-negotiable):
1. Cut a 30cm × 30cm test panel from the front, middle, end of the roll.
2. Apply to glass test rig, power up.
3. Check: switching speed, uniformity, dead zones, haze level, edge integrity.
4. Document with photo + video.
5. If defects exceed 3% surface area, claim against supplier.

### Controllers/transformers — Australian electrical wholesaler
**The critical decision.** You're paying 30–40% more than China-direct, but you're buying:
- RCM compliance (legally required)
- Same-day stock
- A warranty backed by an Australian entity
- No EESS registration burden

**Who to call:** Lawrence & Hanson, MM Electrical Merchandising (MMEM), Rexel, CNW Electrical, Middendorp Electric, Ideal Electrical. Ask their account manager:
- "Do you stock 60V AC Class II transformers + low-voltage smart relays?"
- "Smallest case pack and best trade price?"
- "Do you have a smart home / scene controller that works with 48–65V loads?"

Alternative: find an Australian distributor of an established smart film brand (e.g. Switchable PLC, Smart Film Australia) and buy their controller-only at trade. Some will.

### Freight
- First order: **air freight** (1–2 weeks). You can't afford 6 weeks of no stock to start sales.
- From month 4 onward: **sea freight** in quarterly batches. ~50–70% cheaper per unit.

## 9. The Sample Kit — Your Single Most Important Marketing Asset

You will win more business from physical sample kit drops than from any other channel combined. Build them properly.

### Each kit contains:
- A4-sized working PDLC panel mounted in a clean acrylic frame
- 12V rechargeable battery pack with inline switch (lets the tradie demo it anywhere, no mains needed)
- A4 folder with:
  - Spec sheet (1 page)
  - Trade pricelist (1 page, Tier 1)
  - RCM compliance certificate for the controller
  - "Why your client will love this" leave-behind (1 page, customer-facing)
  - Your card
- Your branded sticker on the frame back
- Reusable carry pouch

**Cost per kit:** ~A$150
**Total budget:** 15 kits × A$150 = A$2,250

### How to use them
- Hand-deliver to every target tradie. Not posted, not couriered. **You walk in.**
- 10 minutes max per visit. Demo the panel, leave the kit, take their card, ask what jobs they have coming up.
- Follow up by SMS 48 hours later — "Hey, [name], dropped the smart film demo with you Tuesday. Got any jobs I can quote for you this week?"
- Follow up again in 2 weeks if no order.

**Rule:** never give a kit to a tradie who isn't a real prospect. Qualify first — Google their business, check their reviews, make sure they actually do glass work.

## 10. Compliance & Legal Must-Dos

| Item | Action |
|---|---|
| ABN, GST registration | Register at business.gov.au. GST mandatory once turnover >A$75k, voluntary before. |
| Business name registration | ASIC, A$44/year or A$102/3 years. |
| Trademark | IP Australia, ~A$330+ per class. Apply early — names get squatted. |
| Public liability insurance | Min A$10m. Quotes from BizCover, Vero, CGU. |
| Product liability insurance | Usually bundled. Confirm covers imported product. |
| RCM mark on controllers | **Non-negotiable.** Only sell controllers/transformers carrying RCM. |
| Australian Consumer Law warranty | Statutory minimum. Offer 3 years on film, 2 years on electronics. Reserve 3% of revenue. |
| Customs broker | Engage one (e.g. WiseTech, Cargo Wise) for any sea freight. ~A$150–250 per shipment. |
| GST + duty on imports | 10% GST on all imports, plus 5% duty on most polymer film. Claim back GST on BAS. |
| Records / accounting | Xero or QuickBooks from day one. ~A$30–60/mo. |

**The single thing that ends your business overnight:** selling a mains-connected transformer without RCM. ACCC will hammer you. Don't do it. Ever.

## 11. 90-Day Launch Plan

### Days 1–15: Foundation
- Register ABN, GST, business name.
- Apply for trademark.
- Get 3 quotes for PL + product liability insurance. Bind cover.
- Open business bank account, Stripe account, Xero.
- Buy domain, set up Gmail business.
- Build target list: 50 tradies in your metro (40 tinters, 10 fitout/builders).
- Contact 4 Alibaba suppliers, order samples (5–10m each).
- Call 3 Australian electrical wholesalers, get controller pricing in writing.

### Days 16–30: Product validation
- Samples arrive. Test each on glass panels. Document switching, haze, defects.
- Pick top 2 suppliers. Place first bulk order (80m² film, air freight).
- Lock in Australian controller supplier; order 25 units.
- Build your test rig + cutting setup.
- Build sample kit prototype. Refine. Build 15 kits.
- Build basic website (1-page is fine to start): logo, "trade supply" positioning, contact form, password-protected pricelist page.

### Days 31–60: Trade outreach
- Stock arrives. QC every roll (photo, video, log).
- Stock controllers + kit components.
- Begin in-person trade visits. **Target: 8 visits per week, ~50 in 6 weeks.**
- Soft launch Instagram: 3 posts/week — demo videos, install pics, before/after.
- First trial sales — likely Tier 1 small orders. Treat each like a wedding cake; if they're unhappy, you fix it overnight.
- Track every interaction in a simple CRM (Notion, Airtable, even a spreadsheet).

### Days 61–90: First repeat business
- Target: 5–8 active trade accounts, 1–3 repeat buyers, A$5–8k revenue month 3.
- Identify your 2–3 champion tradies. Lock in Tier 2 pricing, ask what else they need.
- Reorder film when first batch hits 50% depleted.
- Start collecting installer photos/videos with permission for social proof.
- Get 5 written trade testimonials.

### Day 90 review questions
1. Are you above A$8k/month revenue? If yes — order more stock. If no — diagnose: not enough visits, wrong targets, price too high, or quality issue?
2. Which tradie has bought twice? Why? Replicate that customer profile.
3. Are you working >60 hours/week? You will be. Plan how to batch deliveries, set order cut-off times, and protect 1 day a week off or you'll burn out by month 6.

## 12. Risks at This Budget

| Risk | What it looks like | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Bad film batch wipes your stock | One delaminated roll, no replacements in time | Two suppliers from day one; smaller orders, more often; bench-test every roll |
| One tradie defaults on credit | A$3k unpaid → game over | No credit terms before month 6. Cash only. |
| You sell a non-RCM controller | ACCC notice, recall, fines | Only ever sell RCM-marked AU-sourced gear. Audit every box. |
| Burnout | Solo founder doing 70 hr weeks by month 4 | Hard "no work" day each week. Batch deliveries Tuesday/Friday. |
| Cashflow gap on reorders | First batch sells out before you can pay for the next | Reorder when stock hits 50%, not 20%. Sea freight quarterly once cash allows. |
| Big competitor (Switchglass, Gauzy) opens trade pricing | You lose your edge | Compete on speed, support, and relationships — not price. Your A$20k operation can out-care them every time. |
| One viral bad review on a tradie forum | Trade reputation is small-world | QA every order. Replace any defect within 48 hours, no argument. |

## 13. The North Star

By month 12, the business should look like:

- **12–18 active trade accounts**, ~6 of them buying monthly.
- **A$25–40k revenue/month**, 45–55% gross margin.
- **A$30–50k working capital** in the bank (built from operations, not new investment).
- **One additional SKU** added (likely black/tinted PDLC or 1.8m rolls).
- **One part-time helper** for cutting/packing, freeing you to sell.
- **EESS Responsible Supplier registration in progress** so you can self-import controllers in Year 2 and double your controller margin.

If you're there, Year 2 is national. If you're not, diagnose, narrow further, and don't try to scale on hope.

## 14. The Real Question

You're not building a smart film company. You're building a trade-trusted brand in your city. The product is interchangeable; what your customers buy after the third order is **you** — your reliability, your phone answering on Saturday, your replacement-overnight policy when something goes wrong. Build that, and the rest is logistics.
